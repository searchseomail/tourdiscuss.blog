7 Magical Castles You Can Actually Sleep in Across Europe

Have you ever stood before the towering stone walls of a medieval fortress and wondered what it would feel like to wake up behind them? For most, the history of Europe is something to be viewed from behind a velvet rope, but a select few have realized that the ultimate travel experience involves more than just a tour—it involves living the dream. Staying in a castle allows you to step back in time, trade the sterile walls of a modern hotel for hand-tapestried chambers, and experience the continent through the eyes of nobility.

From the mist-covered hills of Ireland to the sun-drenched vineyards of Italy, Europe is scattered with historic estates that have been lovingly restored into luxury accommodations. Whether you are planning a grand two-week European itinerary or a romantic getaway, spending a night in a castle adds a layer of magic that simply cannot be replicated by any other form of lodging. Below, we explore seven of the most enchanting castles where you can sleep like a king.

1. Ashford Castle, Ireland

Located on the shores of Lough Corrib, Ashford Castle is the gold standard for luxury castle stays. Originally built in the 13th century, this sprawling estate has been expanded over the centuries to become a Five-Star hotel that feels like a living museum. With its sprawling gardens, falconry school, and opulent interiors, it is the perfect place to immerse yourself in the mythic atmosphere of the Irish countryside.

2. Château de Bagnols, France

Nestled in the heart of the Beaujolais wine region, this 13th-century monument is a masterpiece of medieval architecture. Staying here is not just about the room; it is about the experience of the French lifestyle. After a day of exploring, you can enjoy fine dining in the castle’s vaulted cellars. If you enjoy the finer things in life, you might also be interested in our guide on combining French cheeses and wines to elevate your evening.

3. Burg Wernberg, Germany

Perched high above the town of Wernberg-Köblitz, this restored fortress provides a quintessential Bavarian experience. The castle features a blend of historical charm and modern comfort, with thick stone walls that keep the summer heat at bay and a world-class restaurant that serves refined German cuisine. It is a quiet, atmospheric retreat for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

4. Castello di Pavone, Italy

Italy is home to many hidden gems, and this 9th-century castle is one of its most remarkable. Located near Turin, it offers a dramatic setting with ancient courtyards and lush Mediterranean gardens. It is the perfect base for travelers exploring the hidden small towns of Italy that remain off the beaten path for the average tourist.

5. Amberley Castle, England

Located in West Sussex, Amberley Castle is a 900-year-old retreat surrounded by medieval walls and 12 acres of landscaped gardens. With its portcullis entrance and roaring fireplaces, it offers an authentic English country manor experience that feels both grand and incredibly cozy.

6. Parador de Cardona, Spain

This fortress, situated in Catalonia, is a stunning example of medieval military architecture. Perched on a hill overlooking the Cardoner River, the castle dates back to the 9th century. Its high ceilings, stone arches, and panoramic views of the Spanish countryside make it a bucket-list destination for history enthusiasts.

7. Dalhousie Castle, Scotland

Just a short drive from Edinburgh, Dalhousie Castle is the oldest inhabited castle in Scotland. It carries a rich and sometimes haunting history, offering guests a chance to sleep in rooms that have hosted everyone from Mary Queen of Scots to Edward I. It is the epitome of Scottish heritage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are castle hotels always expensive?
While many castle hotels offer luxury suites at a high price point, there are many "Paradores" in Spain and "Pousadas" in Portugal that offer historic castle stays at much more accessible price points.
Do these castles have modern amenities like Wi-Fi?
Yes, most historic hotels have been renovated to include modern luxuries like high-speed Wi-Fi, heating, and en-suite bathrooms, ensuring you don't have to sacrifice comfort for history.
Is it better to book directly or through a travel site?
Booking directly through the castle's official website often provides the best rates, exclusive packages, and a more personalized communication experience regarding your stay.
