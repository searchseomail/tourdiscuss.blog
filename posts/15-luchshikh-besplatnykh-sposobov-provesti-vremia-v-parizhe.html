15 лучших бесплатных способов провести время в Париже

Sarah 20.02.2026 • Reading time: 6 min

15 Free Things to Do in Paris Without Spending a Dime

Paris is frequently romanticized as an expensive playground for the elite, but the truth is far more democratic. If you know where to look, you can experience the magic of the City of Light without opening your wallet. Whether you are a student traveler looking to save money or a savvy tourist trying to stretch your budget, finding 15 Free Things to Do in Paris Without Spending a Dime is entirely possible. From historic parks to world-class art and iconic vistas, the city offers an abundance of cultural riches that are completely complimentary.

1. Explore the Historic Parks and Gardens

Parisian gardens are not just green spaces; they are open-air museums. The Jardin du Luxembourg, with its Medici Fountain and manicured lawns, is perfect for a lazy afternoon. Similarly, the Tuileries Garden, situated between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde, offers a stunning walk through history. For those who enjoy a more wild, romantic landscape, the Parc des Buttes-Chaumont in the 19th arrondissement provides dramatic cliffs, a suspension bridge, and a temple perched on a rocky island—all for free.

2. Admire the Architecture of Notre-Dame and Sacré-Cœur

While some museums charge a hefty entry fee, the most iconic religious landmarks in Paris remain accessible. The exterior architecture of Notre-Dame Cathedral is a masterpiece of Gothic design. Meanwhile, climbing the hill of Montmartre to reach the Basilica of the Sacré-Cœur rewards you with both a stunning interior and one of the best panoramic views of the city. As you plan your trip, remember that if you are looking to travel around Europe on a budget, these landmarks are essential stops that keep your costs down.

3. Wander Through Trendy Neighborhoods

Some of the best experiences in Paris cost nothing but time. Wandering through Le Marais, with its medieval streets, hidden courtyards, and vibrant boutiques, feels like stepping back in time. Similarly, the Latin Quarter offers a glimpse into the intellectual history of the city. If you are a fan of Europe's best cities for travel, you will find that the street life in Paris is unparalleled in its charm and atmosphere.

4. Enjoy World-Class Art Galleries

Many smaller museums in Paris are permanently free. The Musée Carnavalet, dedicated to the history of Paris, is a must-see. Additionally, the Petit Palais offers a permanent collection of fine arts that is free to the public. If you are lucky enough to be in town on the first Sunday of the month, many major museums, including the Musée d'Orsay, open their doors to the public for free.

5. Walk the Promenade Plantée

Before New York had the High Line, Paris had the Promenade Plantée. This elevated park, built on a former railway viaduct, stretches through the 12th arrondissement. It is a peaceful, lush walkway that offers a unique perspective of the city architecture, far above the bustle of the street traffic.

6. Visit the Famous Père Lachaise Cemetery

It sounds unconventional, but a walk through Père Lachaise is one of the most serene experiences in Paris. This sprawling cemetery is the final resting place of legends like Oscar Wilde, Edith Piaf, and Jim Morrison. It is essentially a park filled with beautiful, haunting funerary sculpture and history.

7. Window Shop at Galeries Lafayette

You don't need to buy a designer bag to enjoy the grandeur of Galeries Lafayette. The stained-glass dome is an architectural marvel. Head to the rooftop terrace for a completely free, breathtaking view of the Eiffel Tower and the Opera Garnier.

8. Watch the Eiffel Tower Sparkle

The Eiffel Tower is the symbol of France. While going to the top costs money, watching the tower sparkle for five minutes every hour after sunset from the Trocadéro gardens is completely free and arguably more magical.

9. Relax at the Canal Saint-Martin

For a local experience, grab a bottle of wine and sit by the Canal Saint-Martin. This is where the cool, young Parisians hang out. It’s the perfect place to soak in the atmosphere and enjoy the city like a true resident.

10. Explore the Street Art in Belleville

Belleville is the gritty, artistic heart of Paris. The walls here are constantly changing with incredible murals and graffiti. It’s an ever-evolving outdoor gallery that showcases the contemporary creative spirit of the city.

11. Visit the Arènes de Lutèce

Did you know Paris has Roman ruins? The Arènes de Lutèce is an ancient Roman amphitheater tucked away in the Latin Quarter. It’s a quiet, historic spot perfect for a picnic or a moment of reflection.

12. Walk Along the Seine

The banks of the Seine are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Whether you walk the lower quays or the upper streets, the views of the bridges, the riverboats, and the historic facades are iconic. It is the ultimate free Parisian activity.

13. Discover the Bibliothèque Sainte-Geneviève

If you appreciate stunning interiors, the reading room of this historic library is breathtaking. While it is a working library, you can often step inside to admire the iron architecture that inspired many modern structures.

14. Attend a Free Concert

Many churches in Paris, such as Saint-Eustache or Saint-Germain-des-Prés, host free organ concerts or choral performances. Check their schedules online to catch a piece of musical history in a stunning acoustic environment.

15. Enjoy the Views from Parc de Belleville

For a view that rivals Montmartre but with fewer crowds, head to the Parc de Belleville. It is set on a hill and offers a sweeping, panoramic view of the entire Paris skyline, perfect for sunset photos.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there really many free things to do in Paris?
Yes, Paris has a wealth of free parks, historic sites, architectural wonders, and museums that offer free admission, especially on specific days like the first Sunday of every month.
Is it possible to see the Eiffel Tower for free?
Absolutely. You can view the Eiffel Tower from many vantage points, such as the Trocadéro or the Champ de Mars, without paying a cent.
Are there free walking tours in Paris?
Yes, there are several "free" walking tours that operate on a tip-based model, allowing you to see the city with a guide for whatever amount you choose to pay at the end.
